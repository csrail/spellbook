## Diacriticals

**Breathing marks:** All words starting with a vowel will have diacriticals written above the character.  The closing breathing mark ᾽ indicates soft breathing, therefore say the vowel as it is.  The opening breathing mark ῾ indicates harsh breathing therefore prepend an `H` sound in front of the vowel.\
ἡ $\rightarrow$ HAY\
ἠ $\rightarrow$ AY

<br>

**Accents:** vowels can have accents ´

<br>

**Diaresis:** ¨ markings over a vowel to indicate that it is pronounced separately for example: προϊάπτω is four syllables long, προ-ϊ-απ-τω

## Diphthongs

Diphthongs are combinations of two vowels sounded as one.  There are 9 diphthongs but only 7 sounds.  The redundancy observed in ευ/ηυ and ου/ωυ.  Words beginning with a diphthong have the diacrtical applied to the second vowel.

- αι $~ \leftarrow$ ai from "aisle"
- αυ $~ \leftarrow$ ou from "house"

- ει $~ \leftarrow$ ei from "freight"
- ευ $~ \leftarrow$ eu from French "*fleur*"
- ηυ $~ \leftarrow$ eu from French "*fleur*"

- οι $~ \leftarrow$ oi from "foil"
- ου $~ \leftarrow$ ou from "soup"
- ωυ $~ \leftarrow$ ou from "soup"

- υι $~ \leftarrow$ the French "*oui*"
